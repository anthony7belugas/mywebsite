Overview

Since 2003, the CDC has reported over 950 H5N1 cases globally with a 49% mortality rate. This visualization helps researchers and policymakers identify when, where, and why outbreaks occur, providing critical insights for public health decision-making.

Product Photos

The Problem

Athletes face a hidden performance barrier: just 1¬∞C change in muscle temperature can reduce performance by up to 5%. Traditional shin guards trap heat during gameplay.

Our Solution

Inspired by butterfly wing nanostructures that naturally release heat, we developed the NanoWing Pro ‚Äî a 3D-printed TPU shin guard with biomimetic cooling properties using SEM imagery from Columbia research.

Validation & Results

  • 7 interviews: 5/7 athletes reported significant heat/sweat issues
  • 4/7 said existing ventilation holes are "purely decorative"
  • 100% willing to pay 15-25% premium for cooling tech
  • Heat test: NanoWing Pro cooled faster than competitors
  • Pitched to Ravi Sawhney at RKS Design

Team

Justin Hu, Andy Brennan, Ayla Schorn, Anthony Shen, Ryan Zhu
